  7. Nan & I went small appliance shopping the other day. Normally, we check out Kohl's because of their sales coupons. For awhile Kohl's in Kernersville was honoring their sale price, their coupon discount and their senior discount all on one transaction! That was fantastic! We always thought it was one discount or the other, but, what did we know, and we weren't going to complain! However, the last time we went to their Kernersville store, they told us that we couldn't take all the discounts anymore and that it was one or the other. Oh well. Last week we were in Winston-Salem looking for small electronic appliances and went to the Kohl's there. Before we started shopping, I asked about the discounts and they confirmed that it was either/or, but said that we could also use any "Kohl's Bucks" that we had. We looked at the merchandise and decided that, since there was a Bed, Bath and Beyond(B3) right next door, we would check their prices out before we bought anything. Boy am I glad we did. B3 usually sends out flyers with a 20% coupon per single item on the back. The coupon has an expiration date, but the cashiers will honor any that have expired, so don't throw them away. Kohl's will honor their expired Kohl's Bucks, too, but only in small denominations ($10, $20) at their discretion. We compared B3's prices with Kohl's and were stunned to find that B3 blew them away consistently on small appliances. Even when you added all the discounts in, B3's prices were significantly better on exactly the same brands and model #'s. Both stores were a pleasure to shop in - they had both been updated and upgraded and, appearance-wise, were light and airy. Kohl's generally had a larger selection of small appliances, but Bed, Bath and Beyond certainly had the better prices.

    - Thanks, Tom McCoy Posted 2/27/2012
